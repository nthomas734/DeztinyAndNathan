Travel & Logistics

Getting Here

  • O'Hare (ORD): This is our recommended airport. It is larger and generally has more flight options.
  • Midway (MDW): A great alternative (and often less chaotic), primarily served by Southwest Airlines.

From Airport to Downtown

The easiest and most affordable way to get into the city is via the "L" train.

  • From O'Hare: Take the Blue Line straight into the city (approx. 45-60 mins).
  • From Midway: Take the Orange Line straight into the city (approx. 25-30 mins).
Note: Rideshares (Uber/Lyft) and Taxis are available, but they can be expensive and slow, depending on traffic.

The Museum Campus & Arts

  • The Art Institute of Chicago: World-famous collection (don't miss the Impressionist wing).
  • The Field Museum: Natural history and dinosaurs (home to Sue the T-Rex).
  • Shedd Aquarium: Right on the lakefront; great views and exhibits.
  • Adler Planetarium: For space enthusiasts and the best skyline view in the city.
  • Museum of Science and Industry (MSI): A bit further south, but incredible hands-on exhibits (U-505 Submarine, Coal Mine).
  • More Museums: Chicago is home to over 60 museums covering everything from contemporary art to local history. Browse the full list on Tripadvisor.

Explore Lincoln Park

Since the ceremony is in the park, this is a great area to explore beforehand.

  • Lincoln Park Conservatory: A beautiful glass house with exotic plants—and a special place for us, as it is where we got engaged!
  • Lincoln Park Zoo: One of the few free zoos left in the country.

Get Active

  • Riverwalk & Lakefront Trail: Chicago has an incredible path right along Lake Michigan and the river. It’s perfect for a walk or a run.
  • Rent a Bike: You can easily grab a "Divvy" bike (our bike-share system) and cruise along the water.

Chicago Classics

  • Chicago Style Pizza: The classics are Lou Malnati's, Giordano's, and Gino's East.
    Honest take: Chicago deep-dish is overrated unless you really love cheese.
  • Our Alternative: Try tavern-style pizza (Piece Pizzeria is a good option), Detroit-style at Pequod's Pizza, or just go for classic Neapolitan at Robert's.
  • Chicago Hot Dog: Devil Dawgs, Redhot Ranch, and The Wieners Circle are good options with multiple locations.
  • Italian Beef: Johnnie's Beef is the best, but far. Al's is an easy option for something closer.

Eat & Drink Guide

Tap any neighborhood to see our recommendations.

River North / Gold Coast

Food

  • Gilt Bar: Moody atmosphere, great cocktails and pasta.
  • Tzuco: Incredible upscale Mexican cuisine.
  • Robert's Pizza and Dough Company: Great riverside spot for thin-crust pizza.
  • Sushi-san: Fun, loud, hip sushi spot (great hip-hop playlist).
  • Lil’ Ba-Ba-Reeba: Tapas style, good for groups.
  • 3rd Coast Cafe: A cozy neighborhood spot, great for breakfast/brunch.
  • Devil Dawgs: The spot for a Chicago-style hot dog or late-night food.

Drinks

  • Three Dots and a Dash: World-famous tiki bar in an alleyway basement. Book a reservation in advance.
  • Sparrow: An intimate, 1920s-style lobby bar tucked away in the Gold Coast.
  • Clark Street Ale House: A classic beer garden and ale house.
Wicker Park & Logan Square

Food & Bars

  • Small Bar: Old-school neighborhood bar with a tiny menu focused on their famous burger.
  • Bungalow by Middle Brow: Incredible pizza and bread in a relaxed setting.
  • Gretel: Great burger and whiskey list.
  • Mott St: Asian-fusion sharing plates (the burger here is also legendary).
  • Piece Brewery and Pizzeria: New Haven-style pizza and house-brewed beers.
  • Map Room: A traveler-themed beer bar.
  • Easy Does It: Chill wine bar/patio (great for waiting for Akahoshi).
  • Billy Sunday: High-end cocktails and vintage amaro bar.

Ramen (Our Favorites)

  • Oiistar: Our go-to spot.
  • Akahoshi Ramen: Tip: This is extremely popular. Reservations drop on Mondays for the following week and go instantly. If you can't get a reservation, just walk in, put your name down, and grab a drink at Easy Does It while you wait.
  • Ramen Wasabi: Another excellent option for authentic bowls.
West Loop

Food & Drink

  • Monteverde: One of the best Italian spots in the city. Reservations open 30 days in advance and vanish quickly.
  • Aba: Mediterranean with a massive, beautiful rooftop patio.
  • Trivoli Tavern: Hidden down an alley; cozy, old-school supper club vibes.
  • The Press Room: Intimate wine bar located in a basement.
  • Lazy Bird: A dimly lit cocktail lounge with live music often.
Ukrainian Village

Food & Drink

  • Lao Peng You: Hand-rolled dumplings and noodles.
  • Kasama: A Michelin-starred bakery and restaurant. Tip: The line for pastries/breakfast starts forming before they open. Go early or order online for pickup if possible.
  • Uncle Mike’s Place: Classic Filipino breakfast.
Chinatown

Food & Drink

  • QXY Dumplings: The best soup dumplings (xiaolongbao) in the city.
  • Happy Lamb Hot Pot: Interactive and delicious hot pot.
  • Triple Crown: A classic for Dim Sum.
  • Nine Bar: A cool, hidden speakeasy-style bar.
