Travel & Logistics

Getting Here

  • O'Hare (ORD): This is our recommended airport. It is larger and generally has more flight options.
  • Midway (MDW): A great alternative (and often less chaotic), primarily served by Southwest Airlines.

From Airport to Downtown

The easiest and most affordable way to get into the city is via the "L" train.

  • From O'Hare: Take the Blue Line straight into the city (approx. 45-60 mins).
  • From Midway: Take the Orange Line straight into the city (approx. 25-30 mins).
Note: Rideshares (Uber/Lyft) and Taxis are available, but they can be expensive and slow, depending on traffic.

The Museum Campus & Arts

  • The Art Institute of Chicago: World-famous collection (don't miss the Impressionist wing).
  • The Field Museum: Natural history and dinosaurs (home to Sue the T-Rex).
  • Shedd Aquarium: Right on the lakefront; great views and exhibits.
  • Adler Planetarium: For space enthusiasts and the best skyline view in the city.
  • Museum of Science and Industry (MSI): A bit further south, but incredible hands-on exhibits (U-505 Submarine, Coal Mine).
  • Specialty Museums: Chicago is home to numerous specialty museums covering everything from contemporary art to local history.

Explore Lincoln Park

Since the ceremony is in the park, this is a great area to explore beforehand.

  • Lincoln Park Conservatory: A beautiful glass house with exotic plants‚Äîand a special place for us, as it is where we got engaged!
  • Lincoln Park Zoo: One of the few free zoos left in the country.

Get Active

  • Riverwalk & Lakefront Trail: Chicago has an incredible path right along Lake Michigan and the river. It‚Äôs perfect for a walk or a run.
  • Rent a Bike: You can easily grab a "Divvy" bike (our bike-share system) and cruise along the water.

Chicago Classics

  • Chicago Style Pizza: The classics are Lou Malnati's, Giordano's, and Gino's East.
    Note: Chicago style is overrated, though, unless you really like cheese.
  • Our Alternative: Try tavern-style pizza (Piece Pizzeria is a good option), Detroit-style at Pequod's Pizza, or just go for classic Neapolitan at Robert's.
  • Chicago Hot Dog: Devil Dawgs, Redhot Ranch, and The Wieners Circle are good options with multiple locations.
  • Italian Beef: Johnnie's Beef is the best, but far. Al's is an easy option for something closer.

Ramen (Our Favorites)

  • Oiistar: Our go-to spot.
